Halloween Project

History of Halloween:

  • 500BCE: Samhain (sa win) of Celtic pagan origin was celebrated from Oct 31st to Nov 1st.
    • Special communal bonfires and guising(costumes) were among many parts of the festival.
  • 1000CE: The Christian church made Oct 31st All Hallows Eve, Nov 1st All Hallows Day (aka Hallowmas), and Nov 2nd All Souls' Day.
    • It was celebrated similarly to Samhain and many believe it was an attempt to be a Christian replacement.
  • 19th Century: mass Irish and Scottish immigration to North America brought Halloween to the colonies.
    • Halloween became common in Maryland and the southern colonies.
  • 1920's-1930's: Halloween became a secular community centered holiday with parades and town-wide parties.
  • 1950's: Halloween evlvolved into a holiday geared mainly towards young children.
    • Trick or Treating as we currently know it started to become a tradition.
